Verb Tenses Comparison

Tense Use Verb Conjugation Examples
Simple Present General truths, habits, and routines Base form (he/she/it + s/es) - She walks to school every day.
- They play soccer on weekends.
Present Continuous Actions happening now or around the present time am/is/are + verb + ing - He is reading a book.
- We are watching a movie.
Simple Past Completed actions in the past Verb + ed (regular) / irregular form - She visited Paris last year.
- They ate dinner at 7 PM.
Past Continuous Actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past was/were + verb + ing - I was studying when you called.
- They were playing soccer at 5 PM.
Present Perfect Actions that happened at an unspecified time or started in the past and continue to the present have/has + past participle - She has lived here for five years.
- They have finished their homework.
Present Perfect Continuous Actions that started in the past and are still continuing have/has + been + verb + ing - He has been working here since 2010.
- We have been waiting for an hour.
Past Perfect Actions that were completed before another action in the past had + past participle - She had left before I arrived.
- They had finished dinner when the movie started.
